Biography

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the ProrokLab, at the University of Cambridge. I obtained a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Systems Engineering from the Universidad de Zaragoza in 2025, supervised by Prof. Eduardo Montijano and Prof. Carlos Sagüés. I visited Prof. Nikolay A. Atanasov at the University of California, San Diego, in 2022 and 2024. I am a Fulbright Scholar, a DAAD AInet Fellow and a Research Associate of Corpus Christi college.

Research Interests

Robotics, networked system, control, learning and, occasionally, power electronics.

News

Feb 2026  My PhD thesis has been selected as finalists for the 2025 IEEE RAS Technical Committee on Multi-Robot Systems Best Dissertation Award.

Dec 2025  New preprint. We introduce a neuromorphic control framework that leverages cognitive models of decision-making to resolve spatial indecision, enabling robots to break indecision directly from visual inputs.

Sep 2025  Two new preprints. The first one studies how to command collaborative teams of robots through natural language in real-time, and the second one proposes a novel policy gradient method with self-attention for model-free distributed nonlinear multi-agent games.

Sep 2025  Our paper Estimation of Semiconductor Power Losses Through Automatic Thermal Modeling has been awarded a 2024 IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics (TPEL) Second Place Prize Paper Award.

Sep 2025  One paper and one poster accepted. The paper studies curriculum learning techniques for learning scalable, distributed multi-robot policies and will be presented at ROBOT'25. The poster introduces a novel neuromorphic control for autonomous vision-guided flight and will be presented at NERC'25.

Sep 2025  I am visiting Prof. Oliver Brock and his lab at TU Berlin to explore research problems at the intersection of robotics, biology and collective intelligence.

Jun 2025  One paper accepted at IROS 2025 on how to learn automata representations in latent spaces from observations to do high-level task planning and verification.

Jun 2025  One paper accepted at IEEE T-RO on how to combine physics and MARL to learn scalable distributed multi-robot policies.

May 2025  I gave two talks at CNRS, one at IRISA and Inria Bretagne Atlantique, and the other at UPHF.

Feb 2025  One paper accepted at IEEE T-RO. It presents AVOCADO, an adaptive and optimal collision avoidance method driven by opinion.

Feb 2025  One paper accepted at IEEE TAC: it solves, for the first time, the problem of distributed discrete-time dynamic outer approximation of the intersection of ellipsoids, with a wide range of application in distributed estimation, robust control and computer vision.
